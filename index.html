About Me

I'm a PhD candidate at Michigan State University in the Computational Mathematics, Science, and Engineering Department, specializing in computational metascience. My research focuses on developing AI methods to connect global researchers through a comprehensive network, aiming to unify the worldwide research enterprise.

Research Projects

I work on various projects to connect global researchers, including:

  1. Author name disambiguation algorithms
  2. Studying career trajectories of PhD graduates
  3. Tracking facility usage through machine learning techniques

Research Interests

My interests lie in understanding the collaboration dynamics of researchers across the world and identifying ways to assist researchers using data science. I believe that accurate disambiguation has far-reaching impacts:

  • Facilitating collaborations
  • Identifying emerging experts for conferences
  • Connecting researchers from under-resourced areas

Ultimately, my goal is to contribute to a more interconnected and equitable worldwide research ecosystem.
